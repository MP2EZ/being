# FullMind User Journey Flows & Persona Analysis

## Document Overview

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** January 2025  
**Purpose:** Define how different user personas integrate FullMind into their daily lives  
**Based on:** PRD v1.2, DRD v1.3, Prototype v1.7

---

## Executive Summary

This document maps comprehensive user journeys for FullMind, showing:
- **When** users engage with the app throughout their day
- **Why** they turn to FullMind at specific moments
- **How** they adapt the practices to their real-world constraints
- **What** value they receive from each interaction
- **Where** the app fits into their existing routines and clinical care

### Key Insights

1. **Adaptive Usage Patterns**: Users modify standard flows based on their emotional state, time availability, and environmental context
2. **Crisis Prevention Value**: The app's highest value comes from interrupting negative spirals before they escalate
3. **Integration Over Addition**: Success comes from fitting into existing moments (commute, lunch, bedtime) rather than creating new obligations
4. **Progressive Engagement**: Users evolve from novelty → resistance → integration → mastery over 6+ months

## Primary Personas

### Persona 1: Sarah - The Anxious Professional

**Demographics & Context**
- **Age:** 32
- **Occupation:** Marketing Manager at tech company
- **Location:** Urban, 45-min commute
- **Clinical Profile:** GAD-7 score: 12 (Moderate anxiety)
- **Previous Treatment:** 6 months therapy, considering medication
- **Tech Comfort:** High
- **Primary Goals:** Manage work anxiety, prevent panic attacks, improve sleep

#### Daily Journey Map

##### 5:45 AM - Wake Trigger
- **Context:** Alarm goes off, immediate anxiety flood about the day
- **Emotional State:** Overwhelmed, racing thoughts (anxiety 7/10)
- **Physical State:** Tight chest, shallow breathing
- **App Opportunity:** Push notification already waiting: "Good morning, Sarah. Let's start gently."
- **Decision Point:** 85% likely to engage when notification present

##### 6:00 AM - Morning Check-in
- **Location:** Still in bed or bedroom chair
- **Duration:** 5-7 minutes (target 5, often extends)
- **Flow Engagement:**
  1. **Body Scan:** Notices tight shoulders, clenched jaw, stomach butterflies
  2. **Emotions:** Selects [Anxious, Overwhelmed, Tired, Worried]
  3. **Thoughts:** Acknowledges "I'll never get everything done" and "What if I fail?"
  4. **Metrics:** Sleep 5/10, Energy 4/10, Anxiety 7/10
  5. **Value Focus:** Selects "Peace" as today's anchor
  6. **Intention:** Types "I will take one task at a time and breathe between meetings"
- **Value Delivered:** Awareness before anxiety spiral, grounding before day starts
- **Completion Rate:** 85% (high morning motivation, part of wake-up routine)

##### 7:30 AM - Commute Moment
- **Context:** Train delayed, frustration building
- **Trigger:** Stress response to unexpected disruption
- **Optional Intervention:** Opens app for quick breathing exercise
- **Usage Rate:** 20% of mornings when commute is stressful
- **Alternative:** Sometimes just reviews morning intention

##### 12:45 PM - Midday Reset
- **Location:** Office bathroom stall or building quiet area
- **Trigger:** Calendar notification between back-to-back meetings
- **Duration:** Exactly 3 minutes
- **Flow Engagement:**
  1. **Awareness:** Selects [Stressed, Tired, Focused]
  2. **Breathing:** Follows expanding circle for full minute
  3. **Pleasant Event:** "Good feedback from client on proposal"
  4. **Unpleasant Event:** "Difficult conversation with team member"
  5. **Current Need:** Selects "Space" or "Rest"
- **Value Delivered:** Interrupts rumination, prevents afternoon anxiety buildup
- **Completion Rate:** 70% (sometimes skips when too overwhelmed)
- **Barriers:** Meeting overruns, social awkwardness of leaving

##### 3:00 PM - Crisis Moment (Weekly Occurrence)
- **Trigger:** Panic rising after critical email from boss
- **Physical Symptoms:** Heart racing, sweating, tunnel vision starting
- **Immediate Action:** Taps SOS button (always visible)
- **Crisis Plan Usage:**
  - Reviews personal warning signs (matches current state)
  - Implements coping strategy: 5-4-3-2-1 grounding
  - Considers calling therapist (has number ready)
  - Sets 15-minute timer for response to email
- **Duration:** 5-10 minutes of active management
- **Success Rate:** Prevents full panic attack 80% of time
- **Follow-up:** Often does evening reflection after crisis

##### 8:30 PM - Evening Reflection
- **Location:** Couch after dinner, sometimes in bed
- **Energy State:** Exhausted but mind still racing
- **Duration:** 5-6 minutes
- **Flow Engagement:**
  1. **Day Review:** Mood 6/10, Energy Management 5/10, Values Alignment 7/10
  2. **Pleasant Events:** "Project progress, lunch with friend, good workout"
  3. **Unpleasant Events:** "Morning anxiety spike, deadline pressure, conflict with John"
  4. **Patterns Noticed:** [Catastrophizing, All-or-Nothing thinking]
  5. **Tomorrow Prep:** "Remember: Progress not perfection. One thing at a time."
- **Completion Rate:** 60% (fatigue affects compliance)
- **Skip Pattern:** Most likely to skip on very bad days (when needed most)

#### Weekly Patterns

- **Monday:** All 3 check-ins (fresh start motivation) - 90% completion
- **Tuesday-Thursday:** Morning + occasional midday - 70% completion
- **Friday:** Morning + evening (week review habit) - 75% completion
- **Weekend:** Sporadic - 40% completion rate (less structure, less anxiety)

#### Monthly Arc

- **Week 1:** High engagement post-therapy session, implements therapist suggestions
- **Week 2-3:** Consistent morning practice, midday when remembered
- **Week 4:** Pre-menstrual anxiety spike, uses crisis plan more, completion drops
- **Month-end:** Reviews patterns with therapist, celebrates streaks

#### Long-term Journey (6 months)

1. **Month 1:** Learning phase, trying everything
2. **Month 2:** Finds morning routine essential
3. **Month 3:** Midday becomes automatic
4. **Month 4:** Notices pattern insights valuable
5. **Month 5:** Shares progress with family
6. **Month 6:** Becomes advocate to anxious friends

### Persona 2: Marcus - The Recovering Student

**Demographics & Context**
- **Age:** 22
- **Occupation:** Computer Science major, junior year
- **Location:** University dorm
- **Clinical Profile:** PHQ-9: 15 (Moderately Severe depression)
- **Previous Treatment:** One previous depressive episode, currently in therapy
- **Tech Comfort:** Very high
- **Primary Goals:** Maintain functioning, prevent relapse, graduate

#### Daily Journey Map

##### 10:30 AM - Late Morning Start
- **Context:** Oversleeping pattern, missed morning lectures again
- **Emotional State:** Heavy guilt, low mood (4/10), self-criticism
- **Physical State:** Body feels heavy, no energy
- **First Touch:** Opens app from bed, sees "No judgment, let's start where you are"
- **Engagement Decision:** 50/50 whether proceeds or closes app

##### 10:45 AM - Modified Morning Check-in
- **Adaptation:** Often does abbreviated version (skips what's overwhelming)
- **Focus Areas:**
  - **Body Scan:** Heaviness, fatigue, headache from oversleeping
  - **Emotions:** [Sad, Guilty, Hopeless, Numb]
  - **Thoughts:** Sometimes skips (too painful to acknowledge)
  - **Metrics:** Sleep 4/10 (too much), Energy 3/10, Mood 4/10
  - **Value:** "Growth" selected (aspirational, reminds of goals)
- **Value Delivered:** Non-judgmental start, small accomplishment
- **Completion Rate:** 65% (lower due to depression symptoms)

##### 2:00 PM - Momentum Check
- **Context:** First productive hour of coding homework
- **Trigger:** Notices positive mood shift, wants to capture it
- **Quick Reset Usage:**
  - Focuses on pleasant event: "Finally understood the algorithm"
  - Current need: "Focus" to maintain productive flow
- **Completion Rate:** 40% (only engages when feeling better)
- **Value:** Reinforces positive moments, builds evidence against "I'm useless"

##### 6:00 PM - Social Decision Point
- **Context:** Friends invite to game night
- **Internal Conflict:** Wants isolation but knows connection helps
- **App Usage:** Reviews morning intention, checks weekly patterns
- **Influence:** 30% more likely to engage socially when sees pattern data
- **Outcome Tracking:** Will note in evening whether attended

##### 11:30 PM - Evening Reflection (Primary Practice)
- **Timing:** Before sleep (not ideal but his consistent window)
- **Energy:** More alert at night, easier to engage
- **Modifications:**
  - Emphasis on capturing ANY positive moments
  - Gentle with thought patterns (doesn't judge the patterns)
  - Tomorrow: Sets ONE realistic goal only
- **Value Delivered:** Prevents negative rumination spiral in bed
- **Completion Rate:** 75% (higher than morning)
- **Sleep Impact:** Falls asleep 20 minutes faster with reflection

#### Weekly Patterns

- **High Days (2-3/week):** All check-ins completed, feels productive
- **Medium Days (3-4/week):** Evening only, basic functioning
- **Low Days (1-2/week):** Crisis plan + maybe assessment, survival mode

#### Therapy Integration

- **Pre-Session (weekly):** Exports last 7 days of data
- **In-Session:** Therapist reviews patterns together
- **Post-Session:** Therapist assigns specific exercises in app
- **Between Sessions:** Notes for next therapy in evening reflections

#### Recovery Indicators Tracked

- Morning completion rate increasing (from 30% → 65%)
- Earlier wake times (from noon → 10:30 AM)
- More "pleasant events" logged weekly
- Crisis plan usage decreasing (daily → weekly)
- Social engagement tracking improving

- **Context:** Kids dropped off, sitting in school parking lot
- **Micro-Practice:** 1-minute breathing (not full reset)

- **Context:** 30-minute lunch break at school

- **Full Midday Reset:** Always completes all steps

- **Reality:** No app usage - pure survival mode

#### Values Evolution

- **Ongoing:** Compassion (self-compassion learned)

## Cross-Persona Patterns & Insights

### Universal Engagement Windows

#### Peak Usage Times
- **6:00-7:00 AM:** 40% of all morning check-ins
- **12:00-1:00 PM:** 60% of midday resets
- **8:00-9:00 PM:** 45% of evening reflections
- **10:00-11:00 PM:** 25% of evening reflections (students/night owls)

#### Context-Based Adaptations

**High Stress Days**
- Shortened check-ins (3 minutes vs 5-7)
- More crisis plan access
- Focus on breathing exercises only
- Skip reflection questions

**Good Days**
- Complete all practices
- Explore insights dashboard
- Add detailed journal entries
- Try weekly exercises

**Busy Periods**
- Morning only strategy
- Skip to most needed section
- Quick exports for therapy
- Weekend catch-up sessions

### Journey Evolution Stages

#### Week 1-2: Discovery Phase
- **Behavior:** Trying everything, exploring features
- **Completion:** 80% from novelty
- **Challenge:** Information overwhelm
- **Support Needed:** Simple wins, encouragement

#### Week 3-4: Resistance Phase
- **Behavior:** Engagement dip, shortcuts
- **Completion:** 50% drop typical
- **Challenge:** Habit not formed yet
- **Support Needed:** Flexibility, skip options

#### Week 5-8: Integration Phase
- **Behavior:** Personal routine emerges
- **Completion:** 65% stabilization
- **Challenge:** Consistency
- **Support Needed:** Pattern insights, progress

#### Month 3-6: Maintenance Phase
- **Behavior:** Selective engagement
- **Completion:** Sustainable 60-70%
- **Challenge:** Preventing staleness
- **Support Needed:** New features, community

#### Month 6+: Mastery Phase
- **Behavior:** Intuitive usage
- **Completion:** Personal baseline established
- **Challenge:** Continuing growth
- **Support Needed:** Advanced features, mentorship

## Conclusions

### Primary Success Factors

1. **Adaptive Design:** The app must flex to real-world constraints rather than expecting perfect compliance

2. **Immediate Value:** Each interaction must provide tangible benefit within seconds to minutes

3. **Habit Architecture:** Build on existing routines rather than creating new obligations

4. **Crisis Prevention:** Highest ROI comes from preventing spirals, not just tracking them

5. **Clinical Integration:** Bridge self-care and professional care seamlessly

### Universal Truths Across Personas

- Morning practice has highest completion when part of wake routine
- Midday reset most valued when workplace stress peaks
- Evening reflection completion correlates with better next-day engagement
- Crisis plan usage decreases over time with consistent practice
- 3-6 months required for sustainable habit formation

### Design Imperatives

The app succeeds when it:
- **Meets users where they are** (bathroom, parking lot, bed)
- **Adapts to energy levels** (full practice vs micro-dose)
- **Celebrates small wins** (especially for depression)
- **Provides escape hatches** (skip options reduce guilt)
- **Shows progress subtly** (insights without overwhelm)
- **Maintains clinical integrity** (evidence-based always)
- **Respects user autonomy** (choice and control paramount)

### Final Insight

FullMind works not because it's comprehensive, but because it's **comprehensively adaptive**. It succeeds by being a trusted companion that's there exactly when and how each user needs it - whether that's Sarah's bathroom breathing space, Marcus's late-night reflection, Elena's parking lot pause, or David's disciplined practice.

The app doesn't add to life's burden; it lightens it by providing structure, awareness, and tools precisely when the mind most needs support.
